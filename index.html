Introduction

Two political leaders who hold significant power over their worlds are separated by over two thousand years, yet still use similar tactics to rise and maintain their power. Augustus Caesar built and expanded an entire empire through monuments and architecture, while Trump spreads his influence by promoting his political image, specifically through social media and news outlets. Both leaders understood that political power is not won only on battlefields or ballot boxes, but also through how they craft their public narrative. This project examines the influence of propaganda and image crafting in two leaders separated by over two millennia, comparing and contrasting their strategies for political power.

THE PROJECT

This website is organized into five sections: a thesis, comparison, contrast, conclusion, and bibliography. Altogether, this project will reason that despite Augustus Caesar and Donald Trump being separated by over two thousand years and utilizing different tools to shape their images, their priorities for propaganda reveal the relationship between perception and power.

The purpose of this project is to examine the question of the extent to which the lessons of the Ancient World apply to the issues of our 21st-century world. The project will analyze the topic of the use of propaganda to craft images of political figures, highlighting the similarities and differences over two thousand years.
